Create a 15-slide educational pptx-presentation for an 8-year-old elementary ESL student. The lesson is not the first lesson, so it should include review, practice, and a small amount of new material. Make the slides colorful, playful, simple, and highly visual, with large fonts, short text, and one main task per slide. Use a child-friendly design suitable for young learners. Include speaker-friendly slides that can be used in a live lesson, and make the content interactive and easy to follow.

Topic
Weather, seasons, months, and clothes

Lesson goals
Review weather vocabulary: windy, cloudy, rainy, sunny, snowy, cold, hot, warm.

Review seasons: winter, spring, summer, autumn.

Practice the structure: It’s _ in winter/summer/autumn/spring.

Review clothes vocabulary: trousers, jacket, jumper, longsleeve, mittens, gloves, hood, sweater, t-shirt, shorts, shirt, jeans, scarf, belt, vest, sunglasses.

Practice the question: What do you wear in winter/summer/spring/autumn?

Introduce the 12 months of the year and their abbreviations.

Review the connection between months and seasons. Common season grouping is winter: December-February, spring: March-May, summer: June-August, autumn: September-November.

Slide structure
Title slide
Add the lesson title and a cheerful seasonal image.

Weather review 1
Show pictures and words for sunny, rainy, cloudy, windy.

Weather review 2
Show pictures and words for snowy, cold, hot, warm.

Weather sentence practice
Model: It’s sunny in summer. It’s snowy in winter. Add simple fill-in-the-blank practice.

Seasons review
Show the four seasons with pictures.

Match weather and season
Ask the student to match weather words to seasons.

Clothes review 1
Show labeled pictures of: trousers, jacket, jumper, longsleeve, sweater, shirt.

Clothes review 2
Show labeled pictures of: mittens, gloves, hood, scarf, belt, vest, sunglasses, shorts, jeans.

Boy in winter clothes
Add a picture of a boy wearing winter clothes. Ask: What do you wear in winter?

Boy in summer clothes
Add a picture of a boy wearing summer clothes. Ask: What do you wear in summer?

Boy in spring/autumn clothes
Add a picture of a boy wearing seasonal clothes for spring or autumn. Ask: What do you wear in spring/autumn?

Speaking practice
Use sentence frames:

In winter, I wear .

In summer, I wear .

In autumn, I wear .

In spring, I wear .

Months of the year
Teach: January, February, March, April, May, June, July, August, September, October, November, December.

Month abbreviations
Teach and match: Jan., Feb., Mar., Apr., May, Jun., Jul., Aug., Sept., Oct., Nov., Dec.

Final review game
Create a short quiz or game with 5 questions reviewing weather, seasons, clothes, and months.

Activity requirements
Include a mix of:

Repeat-after-me activities.

Picture naming.

Matching exercises.

Fill-in-the-blank tasks.

Simple speaking prompts.

A final review game.

Design requirements
Use bright, engaging colors.

Keep each slide uncluttered.

Use simple vocabulary and short sentences.

Include clear, child-friendly images.

Add pictures of a boy wearing different clothes on the clothes slides.

Avoid too much text on one slide.

Make it look fun and easy for a young learner.

Important style instructions
The presentation should feel like a teacher-led ESL lesson, not a formal lecture.

Use a consistent visual style across all slides.

Prioritize images over text.

Make the lesson interactive and suitable for an 8-year-old.

Use easy, natural English and clear instructions.
